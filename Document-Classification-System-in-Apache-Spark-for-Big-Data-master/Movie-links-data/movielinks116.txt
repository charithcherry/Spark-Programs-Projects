INHERENT in the manufacture of musical comedy for movies are the minor crises that seem amusing only in retrospect. They proliferate like jungles of cables and lights and have to be cleared from the main body of the performance as remorselessly as the more obvious mistakes such as garbled lines or offpitch singing.
